About Ex-Spec

The Ex-Spec system will be Wrek’d Tech’s flagship modular e-waste recycling architecture. It is intended to combine intelligent sorting, robotics, sensor-based material identification, advanced recovery methods, and data-driven control to improve how electronic waste is processed.

At the system level, Ex-Spec is structured as an integrated 22-module platform spanning intake and preprocessing, hazard mitigation, material recovery, compliance, analytics, and centralized intelligence. The objective will be to allow the full recycling workflow to operate as one coordinated system.

Why It Matters

Electronic waste presents a growing technical, environmental, and resource-recovery challenge. Current e-waste processing methods are often fragmented, labor-intensive, hazardous, and inefficient, resulting in lost recoverable metals, limited material traceability, and unnecessary environmental impact.

Wrek’d Tech will address this challenge by developing advanced systems for safer, more efficient, and more sustainable recovery of valuable materials from discarded electronics.

Partnership Opportunities

Ex-Spec is intended to create opportunities for interdisciplinary academic and industry collaboration across engineering, environmental science, robotics, automation, artificial intelligence, materials recovery, safety validation, digital twin modeling, and circular-economy research.

  • Co-develop interdisciplinary studies and technical publications around modular recycling architecture, systems integration, and advanced material recovery methods.

  • Support phased validation planning for sensing, robotics, and process-control concepts as Ex-Spec progresses through simulation and prototyping stages.

  • Align faculty expertise with applied workstreams in systems engineering, environmental research, and strategic material recovery.

  • Enable structured student participation through capstone projects, graduate research tracks, and supervised technical investigations.

  • Contribute to architecture planning for module integration, data flow, reliability strategy, and staged implementation planning.

  • Advance automation concept development for sorting and material handling workflows intended for safer and more efficient future processing lines.

  • Explore machine-learning, sensing, and perception strategies for future-stage intelligent material-identification and decision-support systems.

  • Investigate sustainability outcomes, emissions-reduction pathways, and environmental performance benchmarks relevant to modular e-waste system design.

  • Study separation and recovery pathways for strategic materials through applied process engineering and data-informed methodology design.

  • Evaluate future-oriented rare-earth recovery concepts and technical trade-offs for circular supply resilience and domestic resource security.

  • Develop safety frameworks and handling methodologies for hazardous components as part of staged technical validation and risk-management planning.

  • Build life-cycle models to estimate potential sustainability impact, circularity outcomes, and future resource-recovery performance scenarios.

  • Study regulatory requirements and compliance pathways to inform future-stage design controls, documentation strategy, and implementation planning.

  • Support curriculum development and workforce pathway planning around robotics, sensing, safety, and circular-economy engineering disciplines.

  • Pursue co-authored grant opportunities to support research, simulation, prototype planning, and institutional collaboration objectives.

  • Coordinate with multi-institution partners on shared standards, research methods, and strategic knowledge exchange for modular e-waste innovation.

Partner institutions will receive an opportunity to participate in an applied, interdisciplinary sustainability technology initiative focused on one of the fastest-growing waste and resource-recovery challenges: electronic waste. Wrek’d Tech will offer faculty and students a real-world platform for research in robotics, AI, sensing, environmental engineering, materials recovery, rare-earth element recovery, and circular-economy systems.
