Advancing modular e-waste recycling through systems engineering, intelligent automation, and strategic material recovery.

About Ex-Spec

The Ex-Spec system will be Wrek’d Tech’s flagship modular e-waste recycling architecture. It is intended to combine intelligent sorting, robotics, sensor-based material identification, advanced recovery methods, and data-driven control to improve how electronic waste is processed.

At the system level, Ex-Spec is structured as an integrated 22-module platform spanning intake and preprocessing, hazard mitigation, material recovery, compliance, analytics, and centralized intelligence. The objective will be to allow the full recycling workflow to operate as one coordinated system.

Why It Matters

Electronic waste presents a growing technical, environmental, and resource-recovery challenge. Current e-waste processing methods are often fragmented, labor-intensive, hazardous, and inefficient, resulting in lost recoverable metals, limited material traceability, and unnecessary environmental impact.

Wrek’d Tech will address this challenge by developing advanced systems for safer, more efficient, and more sustainable recovery of valuable materials from discarded electronics.

Partnership Opportunities

Ex-Spec is intended to create opportunities for interdisciplinary academic and industry collaboration across engineering, environmental science, robotics, automation, artificial intelligence, materials recovery, safety validation, digital twin modeling, and circular-economy research.

  • Access a real-world testbed and shared datasets to co-develop publishable, interdisciplinary studies that accelerate practical solutions for e-waste recovery.

  • Validate prototypes and algorithms at scale using representative waste streams and operational workflows, shortening time-to-deployment.

  • Offer faculty hands-on project opportunities, guest-lecture visibility, and co-authorship on applied research with industry partners.

  • Provide students with internships, thesis topics, and capstone projects that deliver industry-relevant experience and career pathways.

  • Leverage Ex-Spec systems expertise to integrate subsystems, run end-to-end tests, and refine design-for-repair and recovery workflows.

  • Pilot robotic manipulation, sorting, and disassembly solutions in a controlled, repeatable environment to prove reliability and ROI.

  • Co-develop and benchmark sensing, perception, and ML models on labeled e-waste datasets for improved detection and decision-making.

  • Measure emissions, leachate, and other environmental impacts under realistic operating conditions to inform mitigation strategies.

  • Test separation, beneficiation, and process flows to maximize recovery rates and material purity for circular supply chains.

  • Explore targeted extraction methods and process economics for high-value critical materials from electronic components.

  • Develop and validate safe handling protocols, PPE requirements, and automated containment strategies for hazardous e-waste streams.

  • Quantify cradle-to-cradle impacts and model scenarios that demonstrate environmental and economic benefits of recovery pathways.

  • Collaborate on compliance testing, policy analysis, and evidence-based recommendations to inform regulators and standards bodies.

  • Co-create curricula, certification programs, and hands-on training that prepare technicians for circular-economy jobs.

  • Jointly pursue public and private funding with shared proposals, pilot data, and demonstrated operational capability to strengthen award competitiveness.

  • Join multi-stakeholder consortia to share risk, scale pilots, influence standards, and accelerate industry-wide adoption.

Partner institutions will receive an opportunity to participate in an applied, interdisciplinary sustainability technology initiative focused on one of the fastest-growing waste and resource-recovery challenges: electronic waste. Wrek’d Tech will offer faculty and students a real-world platform for research in robotics, AI, sensing, environmental engineering, materials recovery, rare-earth element recovery, and circular-economy systems.

Current Development Stage

Wrek’d Tech is currently in the conceptual and system-design stage. Ex-Spec serves as the company’s flagship innovation concept, with current focus on documentation, technical validation, research partnerships, simulation, prototyping strategy, and staged development rather than full commercial deployment.
